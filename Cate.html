Banana Moon

Background

I'm almost done with an album we've been working on for about nine months. I'm working with this really good music producer named Brian Lenz and we did all the recording in a studio in the attic of the First Covenant Church in Minneapolis. Click here for a picture. If you look at the right of the church, you can see a bit of US Bank Stadium, where they played the Super Bowl this year! Brian is from Carroll, Iowa (just west of Ames) and went to UNI. He's an amazing musician.

We started work on the album in July and just finished tracking (recording all the parts) at the end of March. In total, there are 24 people playing/singing on the album. I do all the lead vocals and all the guitars (except for the extra guitar parts Brian did on "Man with the Candle"). It's amazing what goes into making an album.

Cover Concept

Our idea for an album cover is to take little "tokens" from some of the songs and make some kind of mural. Click here for an example. The center piece would be a banana moon (a crescent moon that looks like a banana) - that's the album title. And then all around it are other items from different songs - a statue from one song, a mortar board (graduation hat), a ferris wheel, a candle, etc., from other songs. Ideas for items are given for each song below. If you want to put the title and my name in the mural somewhere, that'd be good too - otherwise we can add that later. I think you could sketch, draw, paint, use pictures, etc. - no rules!

Recordings

Click here for a link with some really rough mixes of all the songs. Brian has just started mixing so things are all over. In a few cases, we don't have the final tracks in - mostly on some vocals. You'll probably need a good set of headphones and even then the mixes might not be that great. But my hope is you can listen to the songs, read the notes below and get an idea of a cover?

Album Tracks

  1. Oh Academia
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: This is a silly song about higher education. I played in a band in Texas with a guy who was a lawyer and told me "You don't need a reason to sue, just an idea." There was also a fellow faculty member when I worked in Texas who said "I try to be an ass at faculty meetings." (he was)
    • Cover Contribution: I'm thinking this song could be represented by a mortar board, maybe hanging off the bottom end of the banana moon?
  2. In My Dreams
    • Writer:Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: This was written in the late 1990's when I was living in Texas but riding with a work colleague from St. Louis to Champaign. I hadn't been in Illinois in quite some time and my friend didn't talk much so I reminisced about growing up in Illinois. Your mom can tell you about the neighbors and hide and seek and the World's Fair in Colchester. The guy playing accordion is maybe 25 and has 4-5 albums on Spotify. Check him out: John Mark Nelson.
    • Cover Contribution: A baseball field and/or a ferris wheel.
  3. Popeye (featuring Verena Van Fleet)
    • Writer:Lindsay Chase Rose
    • Background: This song was written by a friend from Texas who I used to play shows with. She didn't like a songwriter name John Prine, who I really like. When I moved up here, she sent me this song she wrote and it sounds just like a John Prine song. At the end of the last chorus and the tag, listen for Rachel on the flute!
    • Cover Contribution: We can't use any of the Popeye characters because of copyright issues. But maybe a can of spinach?
  4. Sammy on a Stick
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: Rather than write out a long story (that's what this song needs!), here's a link to a rough version we recorded in May, 2017 that starts with the story of the statue. Click here. There's also an old picture of me and the statue on this site!
    • Cover Contribution: Obviously, we need a statue of Sam Houston. Here is a picture. You can Google Sam Houston statue and get lots more.
  5. Big Fish in a Small Condo
    • Writer: Heather Feronberg and Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: Heather is a cousin of Johnny Nash (he sang I Can See Clearly Now - your mom will know the song). Heather sang with our Texas band and had a little thing for the bass player who was oblivious. We wrote this "missed love" song as an analogy of a fish that got away. There was beer involved. We were stuck on the chorus when she said "Grand Pescado" which is "big fish" in Spanish. Then we were rolling - her mom lived in El Segundo and drove an El Dorado. We couldn't figure out where to put the fish and originally had it as "Big Fish in a Small Pondo" which we decided was dumb. I wondered what Jimmy Buffett would do (your mom can tell you about him) so that's where "Condo" comes from!
    • Cover Contribution: I think a goofy looking fish would be perfect for a contribution from this song!
  6. So Many (featuring Lydia Liza)
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: This was written when Rachel was really little. We were friends with a family that had three daughters whose ages were the same as Sam, Matt and Rachel. The dad was a painter who never had time to paint and I never had time to play music. So the song is about conflicts parents face wanting to be there for their kids but at the same time wanting to have a life of their own too. They had a little sign in their kitchen that said "Babies Never Keep." The oldest daughter is Lydia who goes by the stage name Lydia Liza and is pretty famous up here (she recently opened for the Goo Goo Dolls). She and her boyfriend have had over a million hits on Spotify for their new version of a Christmas song. Click here to watch the video. The violinist (actually it's an octave violin) has play several times at the Kennedy Center in Washington, DC - he's really good!
    • Cover Contribution: We're stumped on finding a contribution from this song. It's okay if not every song has a contribution - maybe things get too busy then? If you have an idea though, that's great!!
  7. Daddy's Pledge
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: I wrote this in Texas right before Sam was born. I was convinced we were going to have a girl so I needed a lullabye song. It took a few tries before Rachel came along!
    • Cover Contribution: The only thing I can thing of is a cloud with lightening and I'm not so sure about that - there's just not much material stuff in this song like the other ones. I'd probably not have anything from this song unless the cover needed a bit more. But if you have ideas, go for it!!
  8. Beacon
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: This was a last minute addition. The song that follows this one is very different than everything else on the album and Brian said he wanted an instrumental segue to get to it. When the album is done, it will roll continuously into the next song. We had interviewed the subject of the next song and Brian pulled out a very powerful clip and tagged it to the end of the instumental I wrote.
    • Cover Contribution: I don't know what this song can contribute since there are no lyrics.
  9. Man with the Candle
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: I wrote this in Texas about a professor (Dennis Longmire) in the Criminal Justice College at Sam Houston State University. He was the only person in the college who was against the death penalty. The university was in Huntsville which is where the main prison system was and where they held the executions (we lived about a mile away). Dennis would hold a candle light vigil at each execution. It was always an upsetting event - some people made signs celebrating the execution and there was a big church right next to the prison with seemed to be a contradiction to me. I spent a lot of time on this song (I have trouble playing the guitar part I wrote!) and it was being considered as background music for a documentary on the A&E channel about Dennis (it was cut out at the end of production).
    • Cover Contribution: I'm thinking the best contribution from this song is a picture of the front of Captain Byrd Cemetery - this is where they bury prisoners who otherwise have no other options. Click here for a picture. You can google it and find other pictures. There could also be a candle.
  10. Learn How to Say Goodbye
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: This is the first real song I ever wrote (1987) after the death of a cousin. I'd moved away from Farmington and was on my own in Southern Illinois. I didn't mind that so much and at the same time I was realizing that the career path I'd chosen meant I probably would never go back there. The second verse is about losing a tennis player friend (Dean Wilson, your mom will know him) and the third verse is about my best boyhood friend. We decided the song should have a mandolin so Brian and I flipped a coin and I lost and had to play it. It took me many tries to get the part right!!
    • Cover Contribution: I'm not sure what we can take from this song? We could use a tennis court but nobody would know what it meant. Maybe a man waving goodbye?? Do you have ideas?
  11. Texas Friends
    • Writer: Pat Van Fleet
    • Background: I wrote this a few years ago when I was missing my friends from Texas. Since it's the last song on the album, we decided to make it a jam song. There are still some open gaps where Brian will add a dobro. The album title comes from the first line of the second verse: "There's a banana moon in the sky tonight." Sam always called a crescent moon a banana moon.
    • Cover Contribution: The banana moon is the contribution from this song.
